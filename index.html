The Next Great HealthTech Innovation Hub

The World Health Organization defines health technology as the "application of organized knowledge and skills in the form of devices, drugs, vaccines, procedures and systems developed to solve a health problem and improve the quality of life. This includes pharmaceuticals, organizational devices, procedures and systems used in the healthcare industry, as well as computer-aided information systems.

Traditionally, Tel Aviv (Israel), Boston (USA) and Cambridge (UK) have become some of the most recognized centers of healthcare innovation in the world. Recently, Barcelona has emerged as the next great innovation center, showing the promise of reaching this pinnacle level in the fight to be the next great innovation benchmark in Europe.

The last few years have shown a significant growth in the number of Catalan start-ups. As a result, custom programs, such as IMAGINE IF, have been exceptionally successful in focusing on supporting innovative projects and maximizing their business potential.

The success of established startups can be primarily attributed to increased access to financial support from national and local governments, as well as collaborative coworking spaces.

The documented track record of success shows that Barcelona is on the brink of an explosion of innovation and the next main target for private or institutional investment. The injection of capital from the private sector could accelerate the growth of Catalan scientific companies and keep Barcelona as the next great hub of innovation.

About Innovation Forum

The Innovation Forum is an accelerator for healthcare ventures. It is supported by a global grassroots network of over 10,000 innovators who seek to improve human health and wellbeing by translating cutting-edge science into innovative products and services.

We focus on the future and the evolution of today’s technologies, which range from the nascent stage to the cusp of commercial application.

Where the Forum Will Be Held

Biotech Health Hub

Hospital de Sant Pau, the home of Barcelona Health Hub sits on a UNESCO world heritage site. The Hospital was founded in 1401 when six small medieval hospitals merged. The modernized campus consists of 26 buildings which have been updated in the late 20th century with the most current technology. In 1991, the hospital was awarded St. George's Cross by the Generalitat de Catalunya.

The main building where much of the innovation and healthcare take place was erected in 2003, to the north of the Domènech i Montaner's Modernista pavilions However, many of the historical buildings are still in use. Departments such as the Blood and Tissue Bank, the radiography department and the physical therapist department remain in some of the old buildings.

Sant Pau hosts a knowledge center formed by relevant institutions in the fields of sustainability, health and education, among others.

These organizations develop their own programs and projects in the Art Nouveau Site, but also undertake joint initiatives with the aim of providing answers to the challenges of the society of the 21st century.
